Problem reframe

The free tier isn't a product.
It's a pipeline — and it's leaking.

Spotify treats free users as ad inventory. But every frustrated exit is a lost premium conversion — worth 10× more than the ad impression they just generated. The fix isn't removing restrictions. It's making restrictions feel fair.

Who we're designing for

Riya — student
Can't afford premium. Needs moments of control, not full unlock. Will engage with effort-based rewards.
Arjun — evaluator
Not price-blocked — value-blocked. Show him what premium feels like and he'll buy it.
Neha — casual
Low loyalty, low frustration. Not converting anytime soon. Keep her in the ecosystem; don't push.

Core design principle

Current model

Restrictions → frustration → exit

Users hit a wall and churn.

→

Proposed model

Friction → agency → habit

Users earn control. Habits form. Premium looks obvious.

4 high-impact features

🎁
Reward listening
Watch 1 opt-in ad → unlock 30 min uninterrupted. User-initiated, not forced. Creates positive ad association.
High reach
🎛️
Daily control window
20 min/day of on-demand play — no shuffle, no skip limits. Enough to taste premium; not enough to replace it.
Conversion driver
✨
Smart ad pacing
Fewer, more relevant ads — spaced to mood context (no ad at the start of a workout, no ad mid-focus session).
Retention
🔬
Premium preview mode
After 3 days of active use, unlock a 48h full-premium trial. Show the diff viscerally — then take it away cleanly.

Trade-offs to manage

Risks

  • Reward system may reduce premium urgency if too generous
  • Daily control window may satisfy power users enough to never convert
  • Increased ad-free minutes = short-term ad revenue dip

Mitigations

  • Cap rewards at 2×/day — scarcity preserves upgrade motive
  • Control window only covers 14% of avg session time
  • Higher ad engagement rate compensates CPM loss
Design north star: Free users should feel respected, not trapped. Every feature earns loyalty first, revenue second.
